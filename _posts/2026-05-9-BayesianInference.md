## Background
Running an A/B test sounds straightforward. Split your test data, measure an outcome, and pick a winner. Yet assumptions in the most common A/B tests can break down in ways that are easy to miss and costly to ignore. Luckily, there are alternative approaches that treat belief as something to be updated as evidence arrives, rather than a threshold to be crossed. In this post, we'll build our knowledge on the Bayesian statistical framework and examine what it really costs to get decisions wrong.
